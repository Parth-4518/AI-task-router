The Strait of Hormuz

The Strait of Hormuz is the ONLY sea passage from the Persian Gulf to the open ocean. It is one of the world's most strategically important choke points, bordered by Iran to the north and the Musandam Peninsula (Oman/UAE) to the south.

Pre-2026 Context

  • Iran and Israel became adversaries after the 1979 Iranian Revolution
  • Iran severed ties with Israel on 18 February 1979
  • Decades of proxy conflict through Hezbollah, Hamas, and Iranian-backed militias
  • Iran's nuclear program and ballistic missile development escalated tensions
  • Before 2026, the strait had NOT generally been closed for extended periods

Escalation Timeline

  • 2023: Middle Eastern crisis began
  • 2024: Iran and Israel exchanged direct missile strikes; Iran seized MSC Aries container ship
  • June 2025: "Twelve-Day War" — Israeli strikes on Iranian facilities; US airstrikes; ceasefire followed
  • January 2026: Iranian security forces massacred thousands of civilians during protests
  • President Trump threatened military action and began largest US military buildup since 2003

Key Events Timeline (2026)

28 February 2026

US and Israel launch surprise airstrikes on Iran targeting military/government sites. Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei and other officials assassinated.

28 February 2026

Iran retaliates with missile/drone strikes on Israel, US bases, and US-allied Gulf states.

28 February 2026

Iran closes the Strait of Hormuz. IRGC warns against passage, boards/attacks merchant ships, lays sea mines.

13 March 2026

Tanker traffic drops ~70%. 150+ ships anchor outside strait.

19 March 2026

US begins aerial campaign "Project Freedom" to reopen Strait of Hormuz. Targets Iranian naval vessels and drones.

23 March 2026

Trump orders US Navy to destroy any Iranian boats laying mines in Hormuz.

27 March 2026

IRGC announces strait is closed to all traffic. Traffic drops to ~zero.

13 April 2026

US imposes naval blockade on Iran after failure of Islamabad Talks ceasefire.

3 May 2026

Trump announces US will help free up ships in Strait beginning next morning.

6 May 2026

Trump announces temporary pause in "Project Freedom" to assess potential for "complete and final agreement" with Iran.

Gulf Allies (UAE, Saudi, Kuwait)

  • Responding with their own strikes on Iran

Pre-Crisis Trade Volumes (2023-2025)

  • 25% of world's seaborne oil trade passed through annually
  • 20% of world's liquefied natural gas (LNG) passed through annually
  • Major route for petroleum products to Europe and Asia
  • Critical for Europe's energy supply
  • ONLY maritime route for UAE, Qatar, Bahrain, Kuwait, and Iraq

Post-Closure Impact

  • Complete disruption of Gulf oil/LNG exports
  • Severe supply shortages for dependent nations
  • Global energy price spike
  • Shipping insurance costs skyrocketed
  • Alternative routes are extremely limited and costly

Affected Countries' Dependencies

  • UAE, Qatar, Bahrain, Kuwait, Iraq: ONLY maritime oil export route
  • Europe: Critical energy supply line disrupted
  • Asia: Major petroleum import route affected

International Relations & Diplomacy

Failed Diplomacy

  • Islamabad Talks: Ceasefire negotiations failed by 13 April 2026
  • Surprise attacks launched DURING ongoing US-Iran nuclear negotiations
  • Assassination of Supreme Leader severely degraded diplomatic channels

US Position

  • Military action prioritized over diplomacy initially
  • Temporary operational pause (6 May) suggests exploring negotiated settlement
  • Maintaining naval blockade as leverage
  • "Complete and final agreement" language implies comprehensive demands

Iranian Position

  • Retaliatory closure of Hormuz as asymmetric response
  • Continued missile/drone strikes despite losses
  • Internal crackdown on protests (January 2026) preceded war
  • Leadership decapitation (Khamenei) creates succession uncertainty

Regional Dynamics

  • Gulf states (UAE, Saudi Arabia, Kuwait) actively participating militarily
  • Shift from proxy conflict to direct multi-state war
  • Risk of broader regional escalation

Global Implications

  • Major energy supply disruption affecting Europe and Asia
  • Potential for global economic recession due to oil price shocks
  • Testing of US extended deterrence commitments
  • Question of whether Hormuz can be militarily reopened against Iranian resistance

Current Status (May 2026)

Strait Status: Effectively closed since 28 February 2026. IRGC maintains closure enforcement.

Military Operations: US aerial campaign ongoing but paused temporarily. Iranian missile/drone capabilities remain active.

Diplomatic Status: No formal ceasefire or resolution in place. Temporary US pause suggests potential diplomatic opening.

Executive Summary

The 2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis represents the most severe disruption of this critical chokepoint in modern history. What began as a US-Israeli preemptive strike campaign against Iran has escalated into a multi-state war with global economic consequences.


The closure of Hormuz — through which 25% of seaborne oil and 20% of LNG flows — threatens energy security for Europe, Asia, and Gulf states themselves. Military efforts to reopen the strait are ongoing but face determined Iranian resistance using asymmetric tactics (mines, drones, missile strikes).


A temporary US operational pause in early May 2026 suggests a potential diplomatic opening, but no ceasefire is in place and the strait remains effectively closed.
